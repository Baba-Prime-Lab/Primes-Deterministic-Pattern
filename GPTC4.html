Proof Concept: The space between any two prime squares in an iterative table inherits more twins and smaller gaps than its predecessor. Starting from the Hexagonal table (6#) and beyond, the quantity grows by a factor of (Previous Quantity * New P). When filtered in the new table, the reduction in total growth does not exceed 1/P. For example, in 7#, the probability columns of 5# repeat 7 times (8 * 7 = 56), and the sieve only removes 1/7th of them (8), leaving 48 survivor columns. This makes it impossible for the new prime sieve to eliminate their existence.
